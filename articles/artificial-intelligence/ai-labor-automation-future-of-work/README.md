# AI, Labor, Automation, and the Future of Work

This repository folder supports the article:

**AI, Labor, Automation, and the Future of Work**

It provides reproducible examples for task-exposure scoring, automation-versus-augmentation analysis, job-quality monitoring, transition-priority scoring, worker-centered governance documentation, workplace AI appeals, training access, worker consultation, and future-of-work analytics.

## Purpose

AI systems do not affect labor only by replacing jobs. They reorganize tasks, skills, autonomy, workplace power, monitoring, evaluation, training, job quality, productivity, and the distribution of gains. This repository treats workplace AI as a governance and institutional-design problem rather than a narrow automation problem.

The examples here examine:

- how to decompose jobs into task bundles;
- how to estimate task-level AI exposure;
- how to distinguish automation pressure from augmentation potential;
- how to identify tasks that require protected human judgment;
- how to monitor job quality, training access, worker voice, and monitoring burden;
- how to identify transition-priority groups;
- how worker consultation, appeals, and governance records can be structured;
- how AI productivity gains can be connected to training, mobility, job quality, and shared benefits.
